Privacy and care

A gentle, clear promise.

The With love, FMB community is meant to feel safe, simple, and human. This page explains what happens when you join and prepare a private message.

What you may share

You may choose a name or nickname, add an email if you wish, and prepare a private message in your community space. Your email is optional.

What stays on your device

Your local profile and the history of messages you prepared are stored in the browser on the device you use. Changing devices, using another browser, or clearing browser data may remove them.

What happens when you send a message

The website opens your email app with the message addressed to withlovefmb@gmail.com. The message is not posted on the website. It is only sent after you review it and tap send in your email app.

Your messages are not public

There is no public freedom wall. Other visitors cannot see what you write. A local copy of the message may remain in your browser history until you remove your profile or clear the browser data.

Your choices

You can edit your display name and optional email inside your profile. You can also remove your local profile and its saved message history from the device at any time.

Support and urgent help

The community space is not an emergency or counseling service. The Help section keeps crisis and mental health contacts visible so support is easier to reach when it is needed.

Joining should feel like entering a caring room, not signing a complicated contract. This guide exists so you can choose with clarity and comfort.
