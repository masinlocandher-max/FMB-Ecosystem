The woman behind the work

Francine Marie Bautista

A Filipina transgender brand strategist, creative director, and advocate from Masinloc, Zambales. A proud Zambaleña and Masinloqueña, she builds brands, stories, learning experiences, and inclusive communities that help people become clearer, stronger, and harder to overlook.

Her work

She helps people see what matters.

Francine works where communication, identity, learning, technology, public perception, and human experience meet.

Francine Marie Bautista turns complex ideas into clear stories, useful systems, and experiences people can understand. She looks beyond appearance. She studies what an audience needs to know, what a project must stand for, and how every part should work together.

Her background in teaching and training shaped the way she leads. She values structure, clear language, and practical learning. Her creative practice shaped the way she sees emotion, visual identity, public perception, and the small details people remember.

She has worked across education, training, photography, visual storytelling, branding, public relations, content, digital products, hosting, writing, and creative direction. These fields support one goal: helping meaningful work become easier to see, trust, and remember.

Her point of view

Clear work can still feel human.

Francine believes strategy should not remove warmth. Good communication can be careful, direct, beautiful, and easy to understand at the same time.

She builds with purpose, protects factual accuracy, and avoids making people feel small just to make a message sound important.

Professional path

A career shaped by teaching, communication, creativity, and advocacy.

Education and training

Francine studied Tourism Management and completed professional teaching units. She worked as an instructor and later as a trainer in corporate and local government settings.

Creative and strategic work

Her work grew into photography, visual storytelling, graphic design, hosting, scriptwriting, content, social media, brand strategy, public relations, and digital products.

Founder-led projects

SENZ Strategic Communications helps people and organizations communicate with greater clarity. Cognita Institute of AI helps learners understand and use artificial intelligence with more confidence and responsibility.

Filipina, Zambaleña, and Masinloqueña

Francine is a Filipina transgender woman and advocate from Masinloc, Zambales. Her identity as a Zambaleña and Masinloqueña informs her commitment to visibility, dignity, inclusive communication, LGBTQIA+ advocacy, and community-centered work.
