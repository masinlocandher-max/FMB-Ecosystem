# Additional Development Instruction: app.francinemariebautista.com

The subdomain `app.francinemariebautista.com` must be developed as a dedicated mental-health and emotional-wellbeing platform under With Love, FMB.

It is not the mobile version of the main website and must not duplicate the full content, navigation, corporate pages, or public bulletin of `francinemariebautista.com`.

## Platform separation

`francinemariebautista.com` is the main public platform of FMB. It contains the official bulletin, portfolio, projects, advocacy work, articles, announcements, and the FMB&CO. company ecosystem.

`app.francinemariebautista.com` is exclusively for the mental-health project.

Do not place the following inside the app:

- FMB&CO. corporate pages
- SENZ services or company information
- Cognita services or company information
- business packages
- corporate booking services
- general portfolio content
- unrelated FMB announcements
- marketing or sales-focused sections

A discreet footer acknowledgment may say:

> A mental-health initiative of With Love, FMB.

## Purpose of the app

The app must provide a calm, private, safe, and supportive digital space where members can check in with themselves, write privately, access helpful resources, and feel accompanied without being overwhelmed.

The experience must feel personal and reassuring, but it must never claim to replace a licensed mental-health professional, doctor, therapist, emergency service, or crisis responder.

## Core app features

The app may include:

- daily emotional check-in
- mood tracking
- private journal with dates
- words of affirmation
- guided reflection prompts
- mental-health reading materials
- music for calm, anxiety relief, and emotional regulation
- personal saved resources
- moderated community posts
- support and wellbeing directory
- crisis and emergency information
- member account and profile
- privacy and account controls
- notifications and gentle reminders
- progress or check-in history

## Navigation

Keep the app navigation focused and simple. Recommended primary sections:

- Home
- Check In
- Journal
- Listen
- Read
- Community
- Support
- Profile

Do not reproduce the full navigation of the main website.

## User experience

The design must be calm, soft, clean, and easy to understand. Avoid corporate layouts, crowded menus, aggressive calls to action, sales language, excessive animations, and visually stressful interfaces.

Use the approved With Love, FMB identity, but adapt it for a quieter mental-health environment. The app should feel connected to the main platform while remaining distinct from the corporate FMB&CO. ecosystem.

Always acknowledge the user’s selected feeling before presenting options. Responses must sound warm, natural, and respectful. Avoid robotic messages, forced positivity, clinical diagnosis, and statements that assume what the user is experiencing.

## Privacy

Mental-health check-ins, journal entries, saved resources, and personal reflections must be private by default.

Do not display journal content publicly.

Do not use private entries as community content.

Do not expose emotional or health-related information to other users.

Clearly explain what information is stored, why it is stored, and how the member can delete it.

Members must have access to:

- delete an individual journal entry
- delete check-in history
- export available personal records
- delete their account
- manage notification preferences

## Community safety

All public community submissions must require moderation or administrator approval before publication.

Provide clear reporting tools for harmful, abusive, threatening, discriminatory, or unsafe content.

Do not present the community as a substitute for professional support.

## Crisis and emergency handling

When a user indicates immediate danger, self-harm, suicide, abuse, or a medical emergency, prioritize crisis guidance over ordinary app content.

Display clear instructions to contact local emergency services, an available crisis hotline, or a trusted person nearby.

The app must not promise that FMB, a volunteer, or an administrator is available at all times.

Include a visible disclaimer explaining that the platform is for support and education and is not an emergency response service.

## Membership and installation

The app may be offered as an installable web application.

After a user signs up and confirms their email, present an optional installation prompt explaining that installation provides a faster, more focused, and convenient member experience.

Installation must remain optional. Users must still be able to access the app through a browser.

## Connection to the main website

The app may include a small link back to the main With Love, FMB platform, but this should not dominate the experience.

The main website may link to the app using a clear label such as:

> Visit the Mental Health Space

Opening the app should feel like entering a focused and protected environment, separate from the public bulletin, portfolio, and corporate sections.

## Final rule

Every feature added to `app.francinemariebautista.com` must answer this question:

> Does this directly support mental health, emotional wellbeing, safe reflection, or access to support?

If the answer is no, the feature belongs on the main With Love, FMB website instead of the mental-health app.
