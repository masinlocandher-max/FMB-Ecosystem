Editorial standard

Clear sourcing. Visible perspective. No manufactured certainty.

01

Sources remain visible

Reports identify the records, releases, institutions and credited images used to build the story.

02

Reporting and perspective are distinct

Verified information is not presented as opinion, and FMB reflection is not disguised as neutral reporting.

03

Corrections remain possible

New evidence can change the record. The newsroom should correct clearly rather than defend an avoidable error.

04

People are not content objects

Images, language and framing must protect dignity, especially when reporting on communities facing harm.

From the founder

Context is not decoration. It is the difference between information and understanding.

FMB News is designed to slow down the parts of a story that deserve care: the source, the people affected, the history around the event and the difference between what is known and what is being argued.
