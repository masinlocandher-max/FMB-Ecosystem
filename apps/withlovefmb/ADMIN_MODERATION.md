# Freedom Wall moderation standard

The Freedom Wall is a member space for things people need to voice out. It is not an unmoderated public dump, emergency service, therapy service, or place for targeting other people.

## Publication flow

1. A verified member submits a post using an alias.
2. The server records the authenticated member, server timestamp, consent evidence, and protected security metadata.
3. The post receives `pending` status.
4. A moderator reviews the post.
5. The moderator approves or rejects it and may add a private moderation note.
6. Only approved posts become publicly visible.

The public feed displays only the approved alias, approved content, and server timestamp. It must not display the member's email address, account ID, IP address, user-agent data, moderation notes, or private journal entries.

## Approve when

A post may be approved when it:

- expresses a personal thought, feeling, experience, hope, fear, frustration, or reflection
- does not identify or target a private individual without lawful and appropriate reason
- does not include another person's private information
- does not contain threats, harassment, hate, doxxing, exploitation, or unlawful content
- does not encourage self-harm or harm to others
- does not present dangerous medical claims as verified advice
- does not advertise scams, spam, or prohibited services
- is suitable for the site's inclusive and supportive purpose

## Reject or hold when

Reject or escalate a post containing:

- credible threats or instructions to harm someone
- identifying information about a victim, minor, private person, or alleged offender
- hate speech or degrading stereotypes
- sexual exploitation or non-consensual sexual content
- graphic violence
- self-harm instructions or imminent-risk language
- impersonation, fraud, spam, or malicious links
- repeated attempts to evade moderation
- unsupported accusations presented as fact
- content that may interfere with an active legal, safety, or safeguarding matter

A rejection should use a short private reason. Do not shame the member.

## Crisis and safeguarding

The Freedom Wall is not monitored continuously. The interface must always say that it is not an emergency channel.

When a submission indicates possible immediate danger:

1. do not publish it
2. preserve only the evidence necessary for a documented safety review
3. show or send the site's public emergency and crisis information where technically and lawfully appropriate
4. escalate to the designated administrator
5. obtain professional or legal guidance before contacting third parties, except where an immediate lawful emergency response is clearly required

Moderators must not diagnose the member or promise confidentiality that the service cannot guarantee.

## Security metadata

Protected logs may include the authenticated account ID, IP address, user-agent information, action, resource ID, and timestamp for abuse prevention and security investigations.

This collection must be disclosed in the Privacy Notice and Membership Agreement. It must not be hidden from members, displayed publicly, or used for unrelated profiling.

Access to raw security events is limited to authorized administrators. Moderators should normally see only the information required to review the post and its history.

## Abuse monitoring

Possible abuse signals include:

- high submission frequency
- repeated rejected content
- coordinated harassment
- repeated attempts to post personal information
- account creation patterns linked to evasion
- automated or scripted submissions
- threats or malicious links

A signal is not automatic proof of abuse. Document the evidence and use proportionate actions.

Possible actions:

- warning
- temporary posting restriction
- temporary account suspension
- permanent removal for serious or repeated violations
- preservation of limited evidence for an active investigation

Members must have a way to question a moderation or suspension decision.

## Moderator conduct

Moderators must:

- protect confidentiality
- avoid copying posts into personal chats or devices
- avoid searching for or contacting members outside the platform without authorization
- keep moderation notes factual and respectful
- disclose conflicts of interest
- use administrator accounts protected by MFA
- report suspected data exposure immediately

## Review records

Record:

- post ID
- moderator account
- decision
- decision timestamp
- private reason or note
- any escalation
- any restriction or suspension applied

Do not place speculative diagnoses, insults, or unnecessary sensitive detail in moderation notes.

## Appeals and correction

Members may request review of a rejected post, account restriction, or inaccurate moderation record through the privacy and support process. Administrators should respond within the published service standard and document the result.

## Periodic review

Review this standard, rejection categories, security access, retention periods, and crisis procedures regularly and after any serious incident. Update the Privacy Notice and obtain renewed consent when a material change requires it.
