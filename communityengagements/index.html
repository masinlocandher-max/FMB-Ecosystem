Ways to take part

Bring the skill you can offer.

Opportunities open as projects become ready. Placement depends on the activity, safeguards, location, and the support available.

01

Community activity support

Help with preparation, welcoming, orderly distribution, participant guidance, and practical tasks during approved activities.

02

Learning and research

Review public information, check sources, support learning materials, and help make useful information easier to understand.

03

Creative documentation

Support consent-based photography, writing, design, and storytelling that protects dignity and never turns service into spectacle.

04

Website and digital support

Test mobile pages, check accessibility, report broken links, organize content, and help maintain simple digital tools.

Work responsiblyFollow the assigned role and never claim professional authority without it.
Reflect and improveReview what worked, what did not, and what the next effort should do better.

Before applying

Care needs structure.

Volunteer work should feel meaningful and safe for everyone involved. Each accepted role should come with a clear task, privacy expectations, relevant guidance, and a person to contact.

  • Be respectful, dependable, and willing to follow instructions.
  • Protect private information and ask before taking or sharing photos.
  • Stay within the role and raise concerns early.
  • Work calmly with people from different backgrounds and identities.

Important: Volunteers are not asked to act as therapists, crisis responders, lawyers, or medical professionals unless properly qualified, formally assigned, and operating within an appropriate service.
