Peer volunteers

Listen and guide

Welcome members, listen without judging, and point people to public resources. Peer volunteers are not counselors and do not handle emergencies alone.

Helpful background:

Community work, student leadership, peer support, social work training, or lived experience handled with care.

Research volunteers

Check facts and sources

Help review reading materials, health resources, cultural records, and historical notes. Sources must be clear and claims must not be invented.

Helpful background:

Research, education, libraries, health communication, history, language, or fact-checking.

Site care volunteers

Test and maintain

Help check broken links, mobile display, accessibility, forms, content updates, and simple technical issues.

Helpful background:

Web development, design, quality testing, accessibility, content management, or documentation.

What volunteers can expect

Clear roles and clear limits.

Volunteers should receive a role description, a contact person, simple training, and a way to report concerns.

No volunteer should be asked to act as a therapist, crisis responder, lawyer, or medical professional unless they are qualified and formally assigned.
